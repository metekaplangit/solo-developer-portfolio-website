**Sole Focus does not collect, transmit, or share any of your data.** It is a
local-first macOS app: there are no accounts, no servers, no cloud sync, no
analytics, no advertising, and no third-party tracking. Nothing you do in the app
ever leaves your Mac.

**What stays on your device.** So your timers and history persist between
launches, the app stores your settings, statistics / session history, task
presets, and window position **only on your Mac** (in its sandbox container).
You can erase all of it from **Settings ▸ General ▸ Danger zone ▸ Reset app**, or
by deleting the app.

**Permissions (both optional).** If you allow **Notifications**, the app shows a
local alert when a focus or break interval ends — generated on your device,
nothing is sent anywhere. If you enable **Launch at login**, macOS starts the app
when you sign in; that's a local system setting with no data involved. The timer
works fully without either permission.

**Children's privacy.** The app collects no data from anyone, including children.
It is rated 4+ with no ads and no external links to unmoderated content.
